Finished making the communications chat dialog in [no signal](no_signal) more interesting.

![image.png](image_1716502724375_0.png)

- I think the thing that makes narrative design good is when the game creates a space of possibilities in which the reader can imagine various outcomes. The game should check the player's hypothesis about what will happen and be satisfying or surprising. However, it should never close the possibility space completely by doing too much foreshadowing or overexplaining because otherwise the reader has nothing left to imagine.
- The new character dialog addresses the signal that the player just saw answering potential hypothesis that the player has about the setting and why the signals are important and explains how some of the crew reacts to them.
- But, it also sets up a situation where the player wants to know more about the stations condition. Why is Bobby fixing things? What was wrong with the LIDAR?
- The dialog is also presented in a way that the player wants to know more. You always start at the end of the chat log, but you want to know how the conversation started.
- It seems like we might not be enticing the player enough, as we don't tell them about the state of the station and why it needs to be fixed. But, after thinking about it I think it's enough to make players curious about the signals.
	- The station's disrepair should be treated as part of the game's environmental storytelling instead of as a goal (fix the station) for the player to achieve.
